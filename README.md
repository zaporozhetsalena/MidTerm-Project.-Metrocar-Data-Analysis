# MidTerm Project. Metrocar Data Analysis
The leadership team at Metrocar, a ride-hailing platform comparable to Uber and Bolt, engaged me to investigate a critical performance issue within their user acquisition and activation funnel.  Metrocar enables users to download the application, register, request a ride, complete trips, process payments, and submit reviews. Despite a healthy volume of app downloads and user registrations, the company has identified a significant drop-off before users reach their first completed ride — a key activation milestone directly tied to revenue generation and long-term retention.  This gap between user acquisition and successful ride completion indicates inefficiencies in the early stages of the customer journey and potential friction points within the product experience.  The primary objective of this analysis was to:  systematically evaluate the end-to-end user funnel, identify and quantify key drop-off points across each stage, uncover underlying behavioral and operational drivers impacting conversion, and develop data-driven, actionable recommendations to improve user activation, optimize the customer experience, and increase overall funnel efficiency.  This analysis is intended to support strategic decision-making across product, marketing, and operations teams, with a focus on improving conversion rates and maximizing the lifetime value of acquired users.
